Kathakali is a classical dance-drama from Kerala, India, known for its elaborate costumes, vibrant makeup, and expressive
storytelling. Combining dance, music, and acting, it depicts epic tales from Indian mythology, such as the Mahabharata and Ramayana.
Kathakali performers use intricate gestures, facial expressions, and body movements to convey emotions,
accompanied by live percussion and vocals. This centuries-old art form is celebrated for its dramatic visual appeal and deep cultural significance.

Origin

Kathakali began in Kerala in the 17th century as a temple art form.

Themes

It portrays epic tales from the Mahabharata and Ramayana.

Costumes

Elaborate costumes and bold makeup define its striking visual style.

Expressions

Detailed expressions and hand gestures convey the story without words.

Music

Live music and drums add energy and drama to the performance.

Culture

Kathakali showcases Kerala's heritage and draws global audiences.
