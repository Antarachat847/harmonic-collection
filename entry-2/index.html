Kantha Art is a traditional embroidery style originating from the rural areas of Bengal and Odisha in India. Known for its simplicity and charm,
Kantha involves stitching intricate patterns and motifs, often depicting nature, daily life, or folklore, onto layers of old fabric or sarees.
This craft transforms recycled textiles into beautifully textured quilts, shawls, and apparel, using running stitches to create a distinctive, handcrafted aesthetic.
Kantha art carries cultural and historical significance, reflecting the creativity and storytelling of the artisans who meticulously craft each piece.
