Komartuli is a renowned artisan hub in Kolkata, India, famous for its intricate clay idol-making.
Nestled along the Hooghly River, it is home to skilled potters who craft stunning idols of deities,
particularly for festivals like Durga Puja. These idols, often created from straw and clay, are celebrated for their artistry,
detail, and cultural significance. Kumartuli not only supplies idols across India but also exports them worldwide,
showcasing the timeless craftsmanship and heritage of Bengal.This artwork has existed for many years and carries the soul of Bengal.

Framework

Bamboo and straw framework to form the basic structure

Clay

Clay- sourced from the riverbanks, are applied to shape and add detail..

Drying

Clay structure is left to dry naturally, ensuring a sturdy base

Detailing

Skilled hands carve intricate details, including facial expressions.

Painting

Idols are painted with vibrant colors to bring them to life
