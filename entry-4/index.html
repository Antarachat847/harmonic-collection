    Kantha is a traditional form of embroidery originating from rural Bengal, practiced predominantly by women. Using layers of old saris and cloth, these artisans create intricate, textured quilts and garments by stitching simple, repetitive patterns like flowers, animals, and geometric designs. The craft is passed down through generations, with each piece telling a personal or cultural story. Kantha artists are known for their meticulous needlework, transforming discarded fabrics into vibrant, one-of-a-kind textiles that blend functionality with artistic expression, reflecting the rich heritage and everyday lives of the artisans.
